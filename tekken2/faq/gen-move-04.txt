1) What's New?-
	 Welcome to the second annual TEKKEN tounament, This time everybody 
from last year's competitors as well as new character's pursuit their own 
reward!  For this year, each competitors have developed new skills and new 
looks! So watch out, many stuffs won't work which did in the first tounament.

2) How many characters?-
	there were only 11 characters available when it first came out, but 
there's actually a time bomb that releases new characters each week ( well, 
actually sub-bosses and last 2 bosses). By this date-Feb 10 1996, everybody 23
characters should be available. The last one who was released was Devil, the 
final boss.

3) How to combo? 10 Hit combos?-
 	combo is a must learn for those who wanna-be-a-master's most combos are 
air combos or sometimes called juggles that hit your opponent several times 
before they literally bite the dust.  To do a air combo, you must have some 
kind of uppercut move which make the opponent fly -high then it's your job to 
find any combo before they hit the ground.  For example, Nina's Split Kick- 
d/b+4,3  sends the opponent up in the air, then you can do short punch or 
punches- either 1 or 2 then jumping kick or 6 hitter 1,2,1,2,1,4 or short 
punch-1 then another split kick just before he/she hits the ground( you 
cannot hit the lifting kick [3] after he/she lands.  Here another, Heihachi 
has two uppercut moves ( d/f+1,2 and f,f+2) after that do a short standing 
jab then do the death punch-hct+2. 
	10 Hitter -  Actually,  those are not combos, just lots of consecutive 
attacks. In another words, they can be broken at a certain point. A beautiful 
thing is there are unblockable attacks are included in those attack 
strings-like Yoshimitsu's. So don't just stand there, break it or run away!
	
	4) why it's so different to other fighting games? 
	one reason is it's has 3-D polygon graphics, most moves are as 
realistic as it should be. there are no jump-in kick, short jab and dragon 
punch combos 
a la Street Fighter. I see many fighting game players give up playing TEKKEN2 
since they cannot do such thing in TEKKEN2. Yes, I admit that it really takes 
some time to get used to this game. Well, it pays, Nobody knows except the 
TEKKEN2players that it has such a depth and high level strategy. So, you are 
the chosen one, guys. Video gaming nobles!
	
	5) Things you gotta know play TEKKEN2
	 number one strategy you have be well known is keeping the distance 
with the opponent because attacks in TEKKEN2 are basically combinations of 
low attacks and high attacks. So there's no way to guard both attacks 
simultaneously. 
	number two, you have to develop your attack style. I recommand you to 
stuck to a character first, and check what you can do, 3 hit puch and kicks, 
high and lower part attacks, throws, 10 hit strings
	And last, not least, check how the computer moves. computer is the main 
source to find out what you can do with the character you picked.
	
	6) In TEKKEN2, you can attack the opponent lying on the ground. You can 
jump and land on him/her, run and step on him/her or just sit and kick. One 
thing before is, unlike TEKKEN 1, the recovering time and moves are varied. 
If you are lying on ground, you can roll aside, quick stand up, low kick 
while getting up or middle kick, roll back and get up, and/or roll back and 
kick low/high while getting up.  So if you want get up as quick as possible, 
just move the joystick up and down, and press 2-right punch repeatedly. One 
thing is that the harder you get damage, the loger it takes time to get up.  
	They are running toward me and tackles me- you have to stop him/her 
before they do; there are two kinds tackle that just crushes on you and 
tackle then falls with you. You can counter the last one by holding joystick 
toward and pressing 2 +4. or just punch/kick them before they hit you.

	7) Useful tips-
	why it's so hard to do 10 hitters?-  to do 10 hitter, you need very 
quick finger work. I figured it out it's near impossible to do it with one 
finger. of course, you need to memorize it first. use the second and middle 
finger ( and thumb if you feel convenient- I think you'd better use thumb 
together when there are 3 or 4 buttons have to be pressed for the first 5 
hits) and do the first 4-5 buttons very fast, but you watcch the screen not 
the buttons- then go for another 2-3 buttons then the last ones. what I 
suggest you is to practice 3, 4 hit combos first.
	Then how can I get out of the 10 hitters- remember, it's not combo, you 
can either break it or escape no matter the first one was good or not. 10 
hitter are mixture of higher and lower attacks; I have found that out when 
the attack pattern switches from higher to lower, or viceversa, computer 
breaks my attack string almost everytime. so keep that in your mind and 
insert your quarters.
	What is the most effective attacks- well, that's a question nobody can 
answer. I think that an effective way against those freakin' button pushers, 
use the lower part attack, and keep a certain distance- you know, somewhere 
they can't kick you, they'll dance crazily for a while, after that, hasta la 
vista, start your attack- death fist, double palm something like that....
	fooling the opponent by get-up and attack- yeah, you gotta know that. 
press 3 while getiing up you'll sweep kick or press 4 to attack mid attack. 
The running-in and stepping on, press 1 repeatedly then you'll roll aside and 
avoid being stepped on. Lei and Nina are the characters who take the best 
advantage of this.
	Multi-throws-  probably the best feature in TEKKEN2, but not everybody 
has it. BTW, you need a right timing to do that. For example, after the 
Nina's palm slap, put 1,3,2,1,1; don't wait 'til she slaps but put that when 
she's doing the first slapping. so it's more like consecutive 
hct+1+2,1,3,2,1,1. then you have more time available to do third one 
2,1,2,3,3+4,3+4 or 3,1,4,1+2,1+2. Actually you can put those code twice; but 
you'll have no time for any second step. try the third step twice for making 
it sure. So the entire button goes hct+1+2,1,3,2,1,1,3,1,4,1+2,1+2-and 
3,1,4,1+2,1+2.
