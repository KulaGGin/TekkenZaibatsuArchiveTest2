~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
                         3. FAQ Description
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~


Ever since Tekken Tag Tournament, I noticed that all available moves
in the Tekken games were not included in the move lists of each
character.  Now that Tekken 4 has been released, I have decided to
take the time to put together a list of all of the moves that I have
noticed that somehow evaded the command lists.  This FAQ only covers
the moves that are missing in the PS2 Tekken 4 (U.S. Version) command
lists.  These moves should work on the Arcade and other PS2 Versions of
Tekken 4, but I only tested these moves on the U.S. Version.

Because Tekken has so many moves that can be linked together, not all
the commands for these moves were included in the move lists.  Listed
in here are moves and button combinations not found specifically in any
of the move lists within the Tekken 4 PS2 game.  Also included are
commands with unique properties.



~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
                            4. Comments
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~


The purpose of this FAQ is to try to inform the Tekken 4 players of the
commands that are available to them, aside from what they have been
provided with in the in-game move lists.  These moves were obtained
various ways: experimenting with button combinations while in practice
mode, learning and trying to perform the same moves used by the computer,
and some just by chance.  A few of the moves were obtained with the help
of "Catlord's Moves List" which can be found at GameFAQs (www.gamefaqs.com),
Tekken Zaibatsu's Movelists (www.tekkenzaibatsu.com), and some Tekken
fans: Tonymitsu and Velari Lockheart.

I have done a little bit of research, and I've found that there are some
moves in this FAQ that cannot be found in any other Tekken 4 Movelist
currently available.  With that said, I hope you find this FAQ useful.

If you happen to see any move(s) listed in this FAQ that are actually
listed in the command lists within the U.S. version of the Tekken 4 PS2
game, please e-mail me and tell me the name of the move and its command
list number.

An example is Lei Wulong's Play Dead: number 016 / 116, which means
command #16 out of 116 total.  The number I need would be just #16.

You will receive credit for noticing commands like this whenever I
create the next version of this FAQ.  You will also receive credit for
noticing any typos or any other mistakes with the commands.  Thank you
in advance.

IMPORTANT: When sending an e-mail, please put something about "Tekken"
or "FAQ" somewhere in the e-mail topic, or the e-mail will be deleted.

                       6. Legend Examples
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~


1) [ ] = Optional command
   
   Bryan Fury
     Vulcan Cannon Body Blow
         D/F#+1, 1, [1], [1], 2

     This move can be expanded to these moves...
        Vulcan Cannon Body Blow 1
             D/F#+1, 1, 2
        Vulcan Cannon Body Blow 2
             D/F#+1, 1, 1, 2
        Vulcan Cannon Body Blow 3
             D/F#+1, 1, 1, 1, 2


2) ( _ ) = or

   Steve Fox
     Left Right Left to Swaying
          1, 2, 1, b+(3 _ 4)

     This move can be performed by doing:
        1, 2, 1, b+3
     or by doing...
        1, 2, 1, b+4
     Only one of these commands are needed.


3) + = and

   Christie Monteiro
     Cabra Macaco Throw (ALT)
          d/f+1+2

     Press "d/f" and "1" and "2" all at the same exact time.


4) DCT and DC2T (Explaination)

   Explanation: You are performing a move that can be transitioned
   into "DC" or "DC2" if you were to press b, f afterwards.

   Marshall Law
     DC2-DC Instant Transition
          DC2T ~ b, f, b, f


5) "Indian Stance Motion Taunt" (Explaination)
   Yoshimitsu
     Indian Stance Motion Taunt
          INS 3+4#, (1 _ 2), (u _ d _ f _ b)
   While in the Yoshimitsu's Indian stance, hold down "3+4".  While
   still holding these buttons, press "1" or "2".  Then while you are
   still holding down "3+4", press any directional D-PAD button.
   You will hear Yoshimitsu's taunt whenever you press any D-PAD
   button.
   
   NOTE1: During this entire hidden move, you will be holding down the
          "3+4" buttons.

   NOTE2: Pressing the "1" or "2" buttons will give you two different
          taunts.

   NOTE3: When Yoshimitsu is wearing his bug outfit, you will know that
          you are doing this hidden move correctly if you see his wings
          retract.


6) AOP BT (Explaination)

   Ling Xiaoyu
     Reverse Phoenix Mistrust
          AOP BT 4

     "AOP BT" means that you are in the AOP stance with your back
     towards your enemy.  This can occur when you are in "AOP"
     and the opponent jumps over you or sidesteps around you.

Headbutt Carnival Counter
Heihachi - Bryan, Heihachi, Jin, Kazuya, Lei, Paul

right, then when he does his "Headbutt Carnival" (f, f+1+4), then the

      connects.


Headbutt Carnival Counter Counter
Bryan, Heihachi, Jin, Kazuya, Lei, Paul - Heihachi
After one of the above fighters on the left counters the "Headbutt
Carnival" of Heihachi, then Heihaci can then press 1 or 2 to counter
with yet another headbutt.  The counters can go on forever, or until
someone dies.
Note: 1 or 2 must be pressed at the exact moment that the headbutt
      connects.


Mach Breakable
Steve - Bryan
When Steve throws a left punch at Bryan Fury, and Bryan parries the
left punch and does his "Parry to Mach Breaker " (b+1+2 {ParL}, 2),
then Steve can hold the back button, B, and then Steve will
automatically go right into his sway to avoid the mach breaker.


Slap Happy
Nina - Christie (Not Eddy), Julia, Ling/Miharu, Nina
When Nina performs her "Slap" (b+2), or her "Double Slap" (b+2, 2)
against one the females above on the right, then they can counter
with their own slap by pressing 2, or 2,2.
Note: The person must be hit by the slap in order for the counter
      to work.


Slap Happy Counter
Christie (Not Eddy), Julia, Ling/Miharu, Nina - Nina
Nina can also counter the countered slap with another slap by
pressing 2, or 2,2.  The counters can go on forever, or until
someone dies.
Note: The person must be hit by the slap in order for the counter
      to work.

=============================
     Unlockable Stages
=============================


Unlock Hon-Maru Stage
---------------------
Beat Tekken Force mode with any fighter on any difficulty setting.


Unlock Statue Stage
-------------------
Beat Story mode with Steve Fox.



=============================
   Characters and Outfits
=============================


Time Release Characters
-----------------------
Characters are unlocked by completing Arcade or Story mode with
different fighters.  The order in which the characters are released
are unlocked:

1) Jin Kazama
2) Lee Chaolan/Violet
3) Nina Williams
4) Lei Wulong
5) Bryan Fury
6) Julia Chang
7) Kuma/Panda
8) Heihachi Mishima
9) Combot


Unlock Miharu
-------------
Beat story mode with Ling Xiaoyu.  Select Miharu by highlighting
Ling and then press the circle button.


Unlock Eddy Gordo
-----------------
Beat story mode with Christie Monteiro.  Select Eddy by
highlighting Christie and then press the triangle button.


Play as Schoolgirl Ling
-----------------------
Beat Story mode or Arcade mode with Ling Xiaoyu.  Highlight Ling
and then press the triangle button.


Play as Panda
-------------
Highlight Kuma and then press the triangle or circle button.


Play as Lee Chaolan / Violet
----------------------------
When playing as Violet in Arcade or Story mode, Violet will
automatically change into Lee for the fight against Heihachi.

When you beat Story mode with Violet, the Violet icon will
change to Lee Chaolan.  Highlight Lee and then press square
or X button to play as Lee. Press triangle or circle button
to play as Violet.

Yoshimitsu can regain health during his Indian Stance Healing

=============================
            Other
=============================


Noise Pollution
---------------
If you hold the down button the on the controller before the match begins,
then you will be able to hear Combot making clanking noises whenever he
is hit or when he blocks.
NOTE: The sound effect ends as soon as the match is over.


Unlock Theater Mode and Replay Data Save
----------------------------------------
Beat story mode with any fighter.


Movie Director
--------------
You can change the order your Game Replays are displayed by pressing
the "square" button after the replay files are loaded from the
memory card, while in under "Replay" in Theater mode.

1) By date and time, starting with the most recent save (Default)
2) Group by fighter on left side (Player 1)
3) Group VS battles together, then CPU battles, by date and time
4) Group by fighter on right side (Player 2)
5) Same as #4
   NOTE: This should be ordered by location, but there must be an
   error in the programming.
6) Go back to #1


What Was That Move?
-------------------
You can display the name of each command/move that you perform in
a match by pressing the "select" button during the match.  Pressing
the "select" button again will disable this option.


In-Game Reset
-------------
Press "start" and "select" simultaneously to return to the Menu
screen at any time, except while in the Options menu.


Move Distribution
-----------------
To view how often you have executed a certain move in the game,
go to the "Command List" and press "L1" or "R1".  This will break
each move down percentage-wise to how often that move was performed
during a match or practice mode.


Command Continuous Playback
---------------------------
In practice mode, you can have a move repeated continuously by first
recording it with the command capture, then by playing it with
"SELECT + SQUARE" buttons.


Repeat Team Battle Results
--------------------------
The results of Team Battle can be repeated by pressing the "select"
button.

----------
Question 1 - Jin's Soul Omen
----------

Q: I can actually perform Jin's soul omen which is b+1+2#, d, u, b, f
   and two of the SOM moves,
   Soul Rage Right Roundhouse Punch f,N,d,d/f+2 and              
   Soul Rage Lunging Low Roundhouse f,N,d,D/F+4
   but I just couldn't perform the other moves although I've tried them
   so many times and I'm sure I follow the exact combo.
   These are the combos that i can't do:
   Soul Rage 5 Hit Stinger Combo
        SOM 1, 2, 3, f+1, 3~3
   Soul Rage 6 Hit Combo
        SOM 1, 2, 3, f+1, 3~3, 3
   Soul Rage 6 Hit Combo to Daemon Soul
        SOM 1, 2, 3, f+1, 3~3, 3 {HIT*}, d+1+2
   Soul Rage 8 Hit Combo
        SOM 1, 2, 3, 1, 3, 2, 1, 4
   Soul Rage 8 Hit Combo to Daemon Soul
        SOM 1, 2, 3, 1, 3, 2, 1, 4, d+1+2
   I saw other people performed the moves though...why is it so??
   i mean can you tell me the exact way and tips of doing the rest of SOM
   moves??


A: First, right after you execute Jin's soul omen "b+1+2#, d, u, b, f",
   you have a window of 5 seconds to perform any Soul Omen with the letters
   "SOM" in front of it.  And as far as I can tell, you can perform any move
   that doesn't have a "SOM" in front of it and still be able to do any "SOM"
   move within the 5 seconds.

   Now, here are some tips on the SOM moves:

   Soul Rage 5 Hit Stinger Combo
        SOM 1, 2, 3, F+1, 3~3
   Soul Rage 6 Hit Combo
        SOM 1, 2, 3, F+1, 3~3, 3
   Soul Rage 6 Hit Combo to Daemon Soul
        SOM 1, 2, 3, F+1, 3~3, 3 {HIT*}, d+1+2
   For these moves, I found it a little difficult to do myself.
   The way that I found that helps a lot is to HOLD down the F
   when you get to it and just keep on holding it until the combo
   is over.  I'll change the "f" to "F" in the next FAQ version.
   Thanks for pointing that out.

   Soul Rage 8 Hit Combo
        SOM 1, 2, 3 * 1, 3, 2, 1, 4
   Soul Rage 8 Hit Combo to Daemon Soul
        SOM 1, 2, 3 * 1, 3, 2, 1, 4, d+1+2
   For the last two moves, I found it helpful if you add a little pause
   between the "1, 2, 3" and the rest of the combo.  Also, for the
   last combo "Soul Rage 8 Hit Combo to Daemon ", it isn't
   necessary to hit the opponent on the last hit to do the d+1+2
   part (like some of the other moves that do require a hit).  Just do
   it right after the last "4" and you should be able to do it.


----------
Question 2 - Ling's Jasmine Blossom
----------

Q: I'm having trouble with the move Jasmine Blossom with the player Ling
   Xiaoyu.  Can you please explain the move in detail as I'm not sure on
   what "while rising" means.


A: I believe that the move that you are referring to is the:
   Jasmine Blossom Wakeup Call
        WS+2 {HOLD} ~ f

   The way I wrote it even seems a bit confusing to me after not seeing
   it for a while.  It should be written as:
   Jasmine Blossom Wakeup Call
        WS+2#, f
   This will be changed in the next FAQ version.

   Now I will explain the "while rising" part.
   While rising is meant to explain the moment when a fighter is in the
   progress of returning to their default fighting position from the
   crouching position, or a move that ends in them crouching.

   You can get to the crouching position by pressing d/b.  By leaving the
   controller neutral, you will go into WS mode.

   There are a number of moves for Ling that will make her go into WS mode
   after performing the move.  Some of these are: d+2 <or> d+4 <or> 4~3.
   After one of these moves are performed, 1/2 to 1 second later, Ling will
   go into WS mode.  This is when you see her on her way back to standing in
   her normal fighting stance.

   Now, for the Jasmine Blossom Wakeup Call "WS+2#, f", when you are
   in WS mode you want to hold down the "2" button.  Just doing this will
   perform the Jasmine Blossom.  But to do the Jasmine Blossom Wakeup Call,
   you want to hold down the "2" button, then press the "f" button when see 
   her moving her right hand.  The move will result in Ling going back to her
   normal stance instead of going to Rain Dance.
