II. Introduction
       At last Tekken 4 was in out at least it was(damn waited a long  
time for it), the ten default characters can only be used. I used Paul 
as my starter character in my first test play. I immediately notice that 
its so hard to block low hits and hard to crouch because now you should 
move the joystick in the d/b position just to block lows and to crouch. 
If you don't you'll keep making a sidestep walk which is the new command 
for a continues sidestep. But to tell you the graphics are sweet. The 
stages are detailed but wait watch out for the walls coz you might get 
trap in your opponents attacks (damn the walls can also hurt you big time). 
       So if you think  my juggles are quite repetitive, we'll i'm just 
trying to be specific on every juggle starter and the move you can connect
because they have different damages on each. Not all juggles in this 
document starts with a juggle launcher some of them might begin with a 
stunner before goes to a juggle or just quick combo moves. So if you think
this is just pure juggles you might want to think again. 
      

III. Controls Layout

Button Command Layout 

Left punch 	- 1          (1)  (2)
Right punch - 2	       
Left kick 	- 3          (3)  (4)
Right kick 	- 4

Controller Command Layout 

  ub  u  uf        f - tap forward             d/f - tap down forward             
    \ | /          b - tap back                d/b - tap down back     	      
 b -- N -- f       d - tap down                u/f - tap down forward    
    / | \          u - tap up                  u/b - tap up back     
  db  d  df      
                                   N - Nuetral   

  UB  U  UF       F - hold forward             D/F - hold down forward
    \ | /         B - hold back                D/B - hold down back
 B -- N -- F      D - hold down                U/F - hold down forward
    / | \         U - hold up                  U/B - hold up back
  DB  D  DF 

                QCF - quarter circle forward     HCF - half circle forward      
                QCB - quarter circle back        HCB - half circle back    

Move Notations    

 + - At the same time
 , - Followed by               
 ~ - Done immediately
 > - Delay              
 * - Hold down move       
[ ] - No hit move       
( ) - Moves done B4 a starter 
__ - Or
CH - Counter Hit

Positions
                       	  	        
FC - Full crouch                        
BK - Back Faces Opponent
WR - While Running
WS - While Rising up from crouch

He has a lot of juggle starters so why not try him. In short Paul is easy and 
powerful while Law has the ease, speed and the moves. If your a female user

   Actually, this part isn't about explaining her juggles, or how to execute
them - resources on my favorite schoolgirl seem to be rather scarce. However,
here are some things you might want to try with her:

   1. Her Double Fan d+1+2 recovers in crouched position (RC). Most of the 
previous guides didn't say this, and you could follow up with a FC or WS move
instead of completing the Fortune Cookie (d+1+2,2>1).
   2. It might not be such a bad idea to start a fight with her Hypnotist 
Stance (b+1+2). Since she could execute any move after going into the stance
except for 2 and 1+2, doing this might put off your opponent off his/her guard
when he plays dumb and rushes in with an attack. Moreover, doing the Spin
Sticker would be ok since it seems quite fast to execute and she avoids some 
attacks during her execution, but the execution does vary with whatever foot
she stepped with. Her unblockable is fine, since you could cancel it, the 
animation causes her to duck and avoid high hits, and it has quite a good
range, but it can be quite a gamble against experienced players. The best
thing to do would be to block the incoming attack, and retaliate with a 
a Storming Flower or a throw.
   3. Instead of simply blocking incoming attacks, why not go into Phoenix
Stance if you know it would avoid it (like deathfists or wind godfists, hehe)?
Doing this gives you an big advantage since her stance changes have very little
recovery time, if there is any, and an AOP move would do just fine, particularly
AOP d+1+2.
