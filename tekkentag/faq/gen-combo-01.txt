 COMBO - A stringing of moves that when the first hit connects, the
 -----   following hits are _COMPLETELY INESCAPABLE_.

=============================================================================

        It is important to remember this definition.. Combos CANNOT be
tech rolled (quick recovered) out of. If you have a Playstation2, you can
make sure your combos are 'true' or 'guaranteed' by setting the Training
Dummy in Practice Mode to "QUICK ROLL /\" or "QUICK ROLL \/".  There are also
a number of stun attacks that can be escaped with a quick tag out or a
controller movement (ex. Tapping forward to escape the hitstun of Jin's
counterhit b,f+2,1,d+2.)  Unfortunately, TTT on the PS2 has no Practice Mode
setting for the computer to escape such stuns.  Remember, if your opponent
can get out of your combo in any way possible, it's not a combo.  

        A bug in the JAPANESE VERSION of TTT is that it reports incorrect
damage percentages when the Counter Attacks setting is turned on.  If you
have a Japanese version of TTT, Do NOT turn on the Counter Attacks setting
because it will count every hit in the juggle as a Counter Hit. In order to
obtain TRUE damage readouts for combos that start off with a Counter Hit,
use the Kiai Tame Powerup (1+2+3+4) to start off the juggle.  Hitting all 4
buttons at once can be a pain in the ass, especially on Playstation pads, so
go to the Controller Setup on the Options menu and bind 1+2 (LP+RP) to the
L1 button, and 3+4 (LK+RK) to the L2 button.  Makes it a LOT easier if you
just have to push down two wing buttons.  Namco fixed this bug in the
American version of TTT.

        ALL these combos have been tested on NORMAL sized characters.  I
used Jin or Julia as the practice dummy in all the combos to verify this.
They do NOT have to be used on big characters like Kuma/Panda, Ogre-2, or
GunJack in order to connect.  In the future I may add combos that work on
big characters, but for now I'm going to concentrate on stuff that works on
normal sized ones.

        To pull off Hard or Very Hard juggles that seem impossible, make
sure to either:
 a) Hit the opponent as early and as high up in the air as possible, or
 b) Move forward between the hits of the juggle, bringing the opponent
    closer (deeper) to you.

		    *-*-*-MOVEMENT CONVENTIONS-*-*-*                        

f - tap forward once           d/f - tap down/forward once
b - tap backwards once         d/b - tap down/back once
d - tap down once              u/f - tap up/forward once
u - tap up once                u/b - tap up/back once

F - Hold stick forward         D/F - Hold stick down/forward
B - Hold stick back            D/B - Hold stick down/back
D - Hold stick down            U/F - Hold stick up/forward
U - Hold stick up              U/B - Hold stick up/back

N - Neutral joystick position (Joystick is not touched)
SS - Sidestep (u,N or d,N)
QCF - Quarter circle forward (Circle stick from down to forward)
QCB - Quarter circle back (Circle stick from down to back)
HCF - Half circle forward (Circle stick from back to down to forward)
HCB - Half circle back (Circle stick from forward to down to back)

		*-*-*-LINKING & SPECIAL CONVENTIONS-*-*-*

+ - Moves must be done together
, - Moves must be done right after the other
~ - Moves must be done IMMEDIATELY after the other
> - Slightly delay the move after the '>'
# - Hold movement preceeding the '#' until end of string or until N (Neutral)

FC - Do move during full crouched position
WS - While standing up (Returning the stick to neutral from a crouch)
cc - Crouch Cancel (Tapping u while crouched to bypass performing a WS move)
BK - Back facing the opponent
FD/FT - Lying on the ground Face Down / Feet Towards opponent
FD/FA -   "           "     Face Down / Feet Away from opponent
FU/FT -   "           "     Face Up / Feet Towards opponent
FU/FA -   "           "     Face Up / Feet Away from opponent

CH - Major Counterhit (Hitting your opponent during their move execution)

Side - Initial juggle starter connects to opponent's side.

[] - Brackets around moves mean that the hits are not part of the combo, but
     are necessary to be thrown for the later juggling hit.

{} - Curved Brackets indicates an attack that misses in a juggle.
